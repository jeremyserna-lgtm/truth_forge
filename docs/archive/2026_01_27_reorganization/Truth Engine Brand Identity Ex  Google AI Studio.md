### PHASE 1: THE CREATIVE BRIEF (Distilled)

Based on your synthesis, here are the non-negotiable rules for the visual identity.

**The Vibe: "Industrial Petroglyph"**  
We want symbols that feel like they were discovered, not designed. They should feel etched, stamped, or forged. If it looks like it could be a slick SaaS app icon on the iOS store, it is **wrong**.

**The Constraints:**

-   **No "AI Blobs":** Absolutely no gradients, amorphous shapes, or glowing blue brains.
    
-   **Monochrome First:** The logos must work perfectly in solid black or stamped into metal. Color is secondary.
    
-   **Tactile:** The final presentation of the logos should always imply texture—brushed aluminum, heavy cardstock, engraved steel.
    
-   **The Unifying Atom:** The Geometric Lightning Bolt.
    

___

### PHASE 2: THE LOGO CONCEPTS (The Three-Part System)

Your synthesis document already perfectly outlined the relationship between the three entities. We will visualize the "Bolt" in three different states of action.

#### 1\. Truth Engine (The Source)

-   **The Concept:** The static, potent center. The unmoved mover.
    
-   **Visual Visualization:** A perfectly vertical, balanced, geometric lightning bolt. To add weight and suggest "wholeness" (the Framework), it could be enclosed within a heavy, industrial circle, like a maker's mark stamped on an engine block.
    

#### 2\. Primitive Engine (The Builder)

-   **The Concept:** Energy applied to raw material. Forging infrastructure.
    
-   **Visual Visualization:** The same geometric bolt, but striking downward. It should impact a horizontal plane or an stylized "anvil" base. It needs to look kinetic and heavy.
    

#### 3\. Credential Atlas (The Seer)

-   **The Concept:** Illumination, verification, outward perception.
    
-   **Visual Visualization:** The geometric bolt radiating outward. This is best achieved by having the bolt emanate from a central point, suggesting a lens, an eye, or a radar sweep. It is energy turned into sight.

### PHASE 4: EVALUATING THE OUTPUTS (Being the Art Director)

You will get dozens of results. Here is how to choose the right ones based on your synthesis doc:

**REJECT if:**

-   It looks like Harry Potter cartoon lightning.
    
-   It has glowing edges or neon effects.
    
-   The lines are too thin or spindly (lacks permanence).
    
-   It looks like it belongs on a crypto website.
    

**SELECT if:**

-   It looks like it could be stamped into the aluminum casing of a Mac Studio.
    
-   It feels slightly aggressive or stark (The "Rebellion").
    
-   The three marks clearly look like siblings (sharing the same geometry).
    
-   It works if you squint at it (strong silhouette).
    

___

### PHASE 5: PULLING IT TOGETHER (Typography & Texture)

Once you have your marks selected from the generation process, combine them with the typography concepts outlined in your synthesis.

**The "Truth Engine" Look:**

-   Take your final black logo mark.
    
-   Place it next to the words **TRUTH ENGINE**.
    
-   Font: Use a characterful Serif (like Instrument Serif or Playfair Display—bold weights) for the main name to provide warmth and humanity.
    
-   Sub-text: Use a clean mono (like JetBrains Mono) for descriptors like "Personal AI System / Unit 01".
    

**Final Presentation Tip:**  
Never present these logos on a plain white digital background. Always mock them up on textures: engraved on dark metal, printed on thick cream paper, or as a subtle deboss on leather. The medium is the message—and your message is "physical reality."
