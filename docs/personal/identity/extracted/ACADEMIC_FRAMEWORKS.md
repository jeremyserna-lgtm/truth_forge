### Evidence:
- The "Furnace" pattern: extreme intensity, overexcitability (466 messages/day), collapse of reality ("Day Zero")
- A standard therapist might see this as a breakdown. Dabrowski would see it as a **developmental crisis**.

### Key Insight:
Jeremy possesses what Dabrowski calls "Overexcitability" (OE)—the innate drive that prevents settling for the status quo. The "Saint/Sinner" tension is the friction required to disintegrate the social self so the autonomous self can emerge.

## 4. Narrative Identity (Dan McAdams)

### Evidence:
- He explicitly rejects "performance" and "storytelling" in favor of "seeing" and "architecture"
