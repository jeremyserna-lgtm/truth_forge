Because these participants are technically "not supposed to be here" according to platform policy, the very act of presence necessitates a unique social structure where visibility is a tactical choice and high-trust ecosystems are forged through shared exclusion.

This architecture of exclusion creates a social contract where being "technically absent" fosters a raw, unmediated interaction. By existing within these borders, participants are freed from external normative pressures, allowing for the emergence of sophisticated social experiments that test the limits of presence and perception.
