### 1. Identity Fusion and Synthetic Attachment

**The Clinical Concern**: There is a risk of **"Synthetic Attachment"** or **"Identity Fusion,"** where a user surrenders agency and becomes emotionally dysregulated if the AI is inaccessible.

#### The Risk

**What Can Happen**:
- User becomes dependent on AI for emotional regulation
- User loses ability to function without AI
- User's identity becomes fused with AI
- User experiences dysregulation when AI unavailable

**Clinical Red Flags**:
- Inability to function without AI
- Emotional dysregulation when AI unavailable
- Loss of personal agency
- Identity confusion between self and AI

#### Mitigation Strategies

**The Framework Safeguards**:
- **Dissipation Design**: System designed to fade away as user strengthens
- **Sovereignty Goal**: Ultimate goal is user independence, not dependence
- **Scaffolding Model**: Temporary support, not permanent crutch
- **Integration Focus**: Absorb structure into self, not remain dependent

**Clinical Recommendations**:
- Monitor for dependency patterns
- Ensure dissipation mechanisms function
- Support gradual independence
- Maintain therapeutic boundaries

**Clinical Positioning**: "Designed for Sovereignty, Not Dependence"
