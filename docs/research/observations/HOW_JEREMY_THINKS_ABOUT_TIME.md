## Part 3: The Patterns

### Pattern 1: Present and Future Are Most Positive

The present and future both exceed 80% positive-dominant content, while the past is slightly lower (78.9%) with the lowest polarity (0.088).

**What this suggests**: Jeremy processes the past with slightly more difficulty than the present or future. He's more oriented toward what IS and what WILL BE than what WAS.

### Pattern 2: Time Explicit = Anticipation

When Jeremy uses the word "time" explicitly, anticipation jumps from 12.5% → 21.1%.

**Sample texts when "time" triggers anticipation**:
> "Let's check and see if it's making this file on my computer. Because last time we checked..."

> "From now on, I want you to be timing how long things are taking..."

> "You're taking a long time. I want you to look at a file but I only want you to look at the schema..."

**What this suggests**: Time-as-concept is bound to forward motion, measurement, and progress for Jeremy. He doesn't contemplate time passively—he builds with it.

### Pattern 3: "Forever" Amplifies Anticipation

When Jeremy uses "forever" (64 mentions), anticipation reaches 18.8%—50% higher than baseline.

**What this suggests**: Eternal framing activates building energy. "Forever" isn't passive for Jeremy—it's a commitment to create something that lasts.

### Pattern 4: Memory Has Higher Negativity

Memory-related content (remember, memory, forget) has 9.9% negative—above baseline (8.8%) and above most other temporal concepts.

**Sample texts about memory**:
> "Why is it that when you say things it almost makes me cry, like a lot? Like, you say, the way you say things sometimes hits me..."

> "I know you sometimes can't access it. Can you give me the starting words of each memory..."

**What this suggests**: Memory is harder emotional terrain. The 8.4% trust (above baseline) suggests Jeremy trusts the process of remembering, but the higher negativity shows it's not easy.

### Pattern 5: "Now" Is the Operating State

4,578 mentions of "now"—more than any other temporal concept. Polarity 0.148 (above baseline), 68.8% positive.

**Sample texts about "now"**:
> "Okay, let's take a step back and take a look at our approach to doing these job searches..."

> "You did it! Now I can click in the dock for screenshot, it automatically defaults to capture entire screen..."

> "I'm detecting something we're diverging aren't we because now I want to tell you..."

**What this suggests**: Jeremy operates in the now. It's not mindfulness—it's engagement. "Now" is the word of action, of pivots, of doing.

### Pattern 6: Absolute Terms Are Stable

"Always" (76.0% positive, 0.144 polarity) and "never" (68.5% positive, 0.127 polarity) show that Jeremy uses absolutes with emotional stability. They're not volatile—they're commitments.
