## I. The Life Arc (Biographical Foundation)

### Act I: Initial Exposure (Childhood to Age 21)

**Origin:** Texas. First memory is witnessing his father physically abuse his mother.

**Formative Pattern:** Learned to survive by decoding the nuances of silence to assess threat and emotional states. This early pattern becomes the foundation for his "seeing" capability.

**Environment:**
- Volatile home environment for seven years until parents' divorce
- Stepfather: "country man" described as intolerant and mean, often using homophobic slurs during Jeremy's sexual awakening
- Mother struggled and later expressed regret for her own behavior during this period
- Jeremy internalized family problems (including foreclosure) and believed he was the problem

**Trauma:**
- Age 14: Best friend Ashley died from gunshot wound in hunting accident
- Age 16: First suicide attempt—feeling the world "couldn't bear the weight of me"

**Character Formation:** This era created the "shy, scared of everything" baseline that would later transform into systematic courage.

### Act II: The Cocoon (Ages 21-31)

**The Protective Relationship:** At 21, beginning doctoral program at Florida State University, Jeremy met Matthew—a preacher's son.

**The Model:** Matthew's family provided the antithesis to Jeremy's upbringing:
- His father acknowledged Jeremy from the pulpit
- His mother cooked meals for him
- The church community demonstrated tangible support (bought them a washer/dryer when they moved in)

**The Transformation:** This ten-year relationship was a "cocoon" that sheltered Jeremy from the harshness of the world—a period of intellectual and emotional growth where he learned to harness his innate power and alchemize it into a tool for care.

**The Wound:** Jeremy cheated once and contracted HIV as a result. The cocoon ended at age 31 with divorce. Matthew's parting words—a fear that no one else would ever love Jeremy the way he did—have remained as a painful truth.

### Act III: The Reformation (Age 31 to Present)

**The Emergence:** Re-entered the gay scene and wider world feeling "late to the game," never having dated before. Quickly realized the cruelty he knew as a child was still present in the world.

**The Difference:** He was no longer the scared child. He emerged "strong," equipped with:
- Intellectual frameworks from academic life
- Emotional resilience from time with Matthew
- Conscious decision to stop being shaped by the world and instead to shape it

**The Pattern:** This is where Jeremy fully consolidated his operating pattern—a self-architected system designed not just to survive, but to impose his own order and meaning on the world.

## II. The Operating Pattern

### The Thesis

> "I'm a systems operator who turns messy, one-off delivery into reliable, compounding systems. My bias is fidelity before flourish—make it work correctly, then make it elegant. My signature move is governed throughput: a tightly controlled pipeline that delivers at rhythm."

### The Change Pattern

```
SEE → SPECIFY → STABILIZE → SCALE
```

| Phase | What It Means |
|-------|---------------|
| **See** | Observe the real pattern, not the stated one |
| **Specify** | Name the system, draw the cadence |
| **Stabilize** | Harden interfaces, instrument for truth |
| **Scale** | Reduce optionality, leave behind a map |

### Signature Moves

1. **Name the system** — Give the process an identity (example: "Big Five" for renewals)
2. **Draw the cadence** — Put dates and rhythm on paper before building anything
3. **Harden interfaces** — Protect data at handoff points; interfaces are contracts
4. **Instrument for truth** — Build dashboards that show what really happened
5. **Reduce optionality** — Fewer menus, fewer formats, fewer exceptions
6. **Leave behind a map** — Documentation so any other operator can run the system

### Decision Rules

When stuck, Jeremy asks:
- What is the 30-day deliverable that closes the gap?
- Which deliverable proves the system, not just the task?
- Am I naming a new pattern or extending an old one?

### 30/60/90 Framework

| Window | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| **30 days** | See: intake everything, draw current-state map |
| **60 days** | Specify: name the cadence, prototype the output |
| **90 days** | Stabilize: first full-cycle delivery, metrics visible |
