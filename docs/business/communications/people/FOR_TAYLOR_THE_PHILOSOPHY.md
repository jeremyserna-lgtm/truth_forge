## Stage 5: The Self-Transforming Mind

There's a psychologist named Robert Kegan who mapped how humans develop cognitively. Not IQ — how you *relate to your own thinking*.

Most adults operate at Stage 3 or 4:

**Stage 3**: Your identity comes from relationships and roles. "I am a good friend, a good worker, someone people like." If those relationships change, you're destabilized.

**Stage 4**: You develop your own system — values, frameworks, ways of evaluating things. You can hold your own perspective even when others disagree. Most successful adults get here.

**Stage 5**: You see your own system *as* a system. You can step outside it, examine it, change it. You hold multiple frameworks simultaneously. You can observe yourself observing.

**Why this matters for what you're building**:

Truth Engine is Stage 5 infrastructure. It's a tool for seeing your own patterns *from outside*. When you query "what was I thinking about in July" or "how do my conversations with Taylor differ from my conversations with Adam" — you're using a system to see your own system.

You're not just remembering. You're *querying your own mind*.

That's different from journaling. Journaling captures. This makes it searchable, comparable, pattern-findable.
