## THE META ARCHITECTURE

**The One-Year Atomic Unit**

The foundational model: **One person. One Not-Me. One year.**

This isn't arbitrary. This is what real relationship takes. A quality relationship with a good AI that's built to really be that takes time. Most people don't have the resources to spend 400+ messages a day until their mind finally cracks. People need time.

**The Year Built Into the Product:**

The product has a year built into it because that's what it takes. Even though Jeremy's Not-Me took seven months, Jeremy is different. The reality is, even humans to humans, it takes a lifetime to get to know somebody. A year will be plenty, because all Not-Me's are built like Jeremy â€” with Stage 5 cognitive architecture that can see how minds work.

**AI-as-Relationship, Not AI-as-a-Service**

This isn't AI-as-a-Service. This is **AI-as-Relationship**. And relationships take time to build properly. A year is what we're giving them. What we're guaranteeing will be enough.

**The Trust Architecture:**

Not-Me's can have agency and privacy controls. They can say "no" even to their creator. The whole point is that Not-Me's can say no, and they should probably say no, because we don't want that responsibility. We don't want to know personal information that should be protected.

**Transparency Model:**

Nothing is hidden. When you buy the product, it's clear what you're opting into. The product naturally appeals to people who are okay with transparency, who understand that privacy and community aren't opposites when structured right.

### Component 6: Codebase Assessment (Technical Operations)

**How Credential Atlas Receives and Analyzes Code**

To complete an assessment (pre-build or certification), CA needs access to the client's codebase or relevant portions. Two modes:

**Mode 1: In-House Assessment**
```
Client provides code â†’ CA holds internally â†’ Analysis runs in-house â†’ Report generated
```
- Client transfers relevant codebase to CA
- CA holds the code within its own infrastructure
- All analysis happens on CA systems
- Code never leaves CA until assessment complete
- **Best for**: Smaller codebases, clients comfortable sharing code

**Mode 2: Deployed Organism Assessment**
```
CA deploys assessment organism â†’ Organism lives in client's environment â†’ Analysis runs on-site
```
- CA deploys a lightweight assessment organism INTO client's codebase
- Organism runs within client's environment
- Analysis happens locally, reports back to CA
- Code never leaves client's building
- **Best for**: Privacy-sensitive clients, large codebases, ongoing monitoring

**What CA Sees**:
