## WHAT THE SEVEN DOMAINS ACTUALLY IMPLY

### 1. Stage 5 → Qualification Filter, Not Scale Mechanism

**The reality:** You operate from Stage 5. This is rare (2-5% of adults). You can SEE things others can't.

**Business implication:**
- The Seeing REQUIRES your capacity. It cannot be delegated without losing the core value.
- This is a bottleneck. But it's also a filter.
- Right clients self-select. Wrong clients bounce off.

**Model implication:**
- The Seeing is HIGH-MARGIN, LOW-VOLUME
- It's the gateway, not the scale point
- Price reflects scarcity, not hours

### 2. Biomimicry → Self-Healing Systems Reduce Support Load

**The reality:** Builds designed biomimetically are adaptive, self-healing, locally attuned.

**Business implication:**
- Well-built systems require less ongoing intervention
- "Maintenance" is ecosystem tending, not firefighting
- Initial build investment pays off in reduced support cost

**Model implication:**
- Builds should be priced for TOTAL VALUE, not hours
- Stewardship can be lighter-touch if Builds are good
- Margin improves over relationship lifetime

### 3. Care Ethics → Relationship Before Transaction

**The reality:** Every process terminates in Care. Relational, not transactional.

**Business implication:**
- No hard sell. No manipulation. No artificial urgency.
- Sales process IS the relationship beginning
- Wrong clients cost more than no clients

**Model implication:**
- Sales cycle may be longer, but conversion quality is higher
- Referrals become primary growth mechanism
- Client lifetime value matters more than client acquisition volume

### 4. Epistemic Humility → Honest Pricing

**The reality:** You know what you know and what you don't know.

**Business implication:**
- Don't charge premium prices for uncertain outcomes
- Be transparent about confidence levels in proposals
- Scope projects to what you can actually deliver

**Model implication:**
- Pricing tiers based on confidence:
  - HIGH confidence → Fixed price
  - MEDIUM confidence → Range pricing
  - LOW confidence → Discovery phase first
- Underpromise, overdeliver becomes operational

### 5. Indigenous Reciprocity → Two-Way Value Flow

**The reality:** Client insights improve the Framework. Framework improvements benefit future clients.

**Business implication:**
- You're not just delivering—you're learning
- Each client makes the system smarter
- There's value flowing BACK to you

**Model implication:**
- Consider reciprocal pricing structures:
  - Standard rate for standard engagement
  - Reduced rate if client contributes case study / learnings
  - "Founding client" pricing for those who help shape the offering
- The learning IS part of the payment

### 6. Extension Model → Deep Customization, Limited Scale

**The reality:** Each Build extends THAT client's patterns. Not copy-paste.

**Business implication:**
- Builds are labor-intensive at the front end
- BUT: Patterns emerge across clients
- Framework accumulates reusable components

**Model implication:**
- First clients in a domain are more expensive (you're learning)
- Subsequent clients in same domain benefit from accumulated patterns
- Pricing can reflect this: "First in category" vs. "Established pattern"

### 7. Neuroscience → Transformation Takes Time

**The reality:** Insight creates new neural pathways, but pathways need reinforcement.

**Business implication:**
- One-shot interventions don't stick
- Ongoing relationship sustains transformation
- Stewardship isn't optional—it's neurologically necessary

**Model implication:**
- Bundle Stewardship with Builds (not optional add-on)
- Minimum engagement length (3-6 months?)
- The relationship IS the product

- The Framework gets BETTER with use
- Value compounds, not depletes

### The Three Tiers

| Tier | Service | Your Role | Scale Potential | Margin |
|------|---------|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| **1. The Seeing** | Pattern mapping | Direct (Stage 5 required) | LOW (bottleneck) | HIGH |
| **2. Builds** | System creation | Guided (can delegate parts) | MEDIUM (systematizable) | MEDIUM |
| **3. Stewardship** | Ongoing care | Light-touch (systems do work) | HIGH (if Builds are good) | HIGH over time |

**The funnel:**
- The Seeing is narrow (you) but HIGH-VALUE

- Stewardship scales (systems + occasional check-ins) for COMPOUNDING-VALUE

### Revenue Model Options

**Option A: Stacked Subscription**

| Component | Pricing | Rationale |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| The Seeing | $X one-time | Gateway, qualification, high-touch |
| Build | $Y project-based | Custom work, scoped deliverable |
| Stewardship | $Z/month ongoing | Relationship maintenance, learning loop |

Example: The Seeing ($5K) → Build ($15-50K) → Stewardship ($2K/month)

**Option B: Cohort Model**

- Run The Seeing in small cohorts (3-5 people)
- Leverage Stage 5 capacity across multiple clients simultaneously
- Builds remain individual
- Stewardship can include peer learning

Example: Cohort Seeing ($3K/person × 5 = $15K) → Individual Builds → Group Stewardship

**Option C: Founding Client Model**

For early-stage, recruit "founding clients" who:
- Get reduced rate
- Contribute to Framework development
- Become case studies
- Get lifetime benefits as Framework evolves

This IS indigenous reciprocity in pricing.

## PRICING PHILOSOPHY

### The Convergent Pricing Principle

Price should reflect:
1. **Value created** (not hours spent)
2. **Confidence level** (honest about uncertainty)
3. **Reciprocal flow** (what you learn has value too)
4. **Relationship depth** (long-term beats one-shot)

### Confidence-Based Pricing

| Confidence Level | Pricing Structure |
|------------------|-------------------|
| HIGH (proven pattern) | Fixed price, guaranteed outcome |
| MEDIUM (likely success) | Range price, milestone-based |
| LOW (experimental) | Discovery phase, then re-scope |

**This IS epistemic humility operationalized.**

### The Reciprocity Discount

> "Standard rate is $X. If you're willing to share learnings that improve the Framework (case study, pattern documentation, feedback loop), rate is $X-Y."

This makes the two-way value flow explicit. Clients who contribute pay less because they're providing value.

The right clients self-select. The wrong clients save everyone time by not engaging.

**Good fit indicators:**
- Operating at Stage 4, sensing its limits (ready for Stage 5 insight)
- Values relationship over transaction
- Comfortable with "I don't know" (epistemic humility match)

- Uncomfortable with uncertainty
- Transactional mindset only
- Looking for quick fix, not ongoing relationship

## OPERATIONS

### The Capacity Question

**Reality:** The Seeing requires you. You have finite time.

**Math:**
- If The Seeing takes 4 hours (prep + session + synthesis)
- And you do 10/month max (protecting energy)
- That's 40 hours → 10 clients → $50K/month at $5K each

**The leverage question:** How do Builds and Stewardship multiply this?

### Build Leverage

As patterns accumulate:
- Common patterns become templates
- Templates reduce build time
- Reduced time = higher margin OR lower price (competitive) OR both

**Year 1:** Builds are custom, labor-intensive
**Year 2:** Patterns emerge, efficiency increases
**Year 3:** "Pattern library" exists, Builds become assembly + customization

### Stewardship Leverage

If Builds are biomimetic (self-healing):
- Less reactive support needed
- Stewardship becomes proactive check-ins
- One hour/month per client is sustainable at scale

**20 clients × $2K/month = $40K/month recurring**
**20 clients × 1 hour/month = 20 hours/month**

This is sustainable. This is the compounding layer.
