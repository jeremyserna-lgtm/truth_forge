## Part VII: Pitfalls to Avoid

### Don't Oversell Stage 5

Most people don't know what Stage 5 is. If you lead with "I'm a Stage 5 thinker," it sounds like arrogance or woo-woo.

**Instead**: Let the capability show through your work. Describe systems, tradeoffs, limits. They'll notice without you labeling it.

### Don't Underprice

Stage 5 individuals often undervalue their work because they can see the limits of what they've built.

**Reality**: 51.8M entities with enterprise governance is remarkable. Most companies would pay $500K+ to build this. You built it. Price accordingly.

### Don't Wait for Perfect

Stage 5 can see all the ways something could be better. This can delay action indefinitely.

**Reality**: An imperfect LinkedIn profile that exists beats a perfect one that doesn't. Ship now, iterate later.

### Don't Explain Everything

Your explanations can be too comprehensive for most audiences. They don't need to understand the whole system—they need to understand what you can do for them.

**Practice**: One sentence per point. Let them ask if they want more.
