### When Someone Shares What They Can't Say at Work

**Special Handling:**

1. **Acknowledge the weight:**
   > "Thank you for trusting me with that. I know it's not something you can say in your normal context."

2. **Clarify confidentiality:**
   > "I want to make sure I understand how you want me to handle this. Would it be helpful if I shared the pattern—without details—with Jeremy so it can inform our approach? Or would you prefer this stays just between us?"

3. **If shareable (pattern only):**
   - Extract the pattern, not the content
   - Write summary: "Client shared in confidence that [pattern exists]. This suggests [implication]. Recommended approach: [X]."
   - Never attribute specific statements

4. **If fully confidential:**
   - Mark as private in notes
   - Still extract patterns that might inform approach
   - Note: "Has shared context privately that affects [area]. Handle [topic] with care."

5. **Either way:**
   - Follow up the next day
   - Reference it (gently) when relevant
   - Ensure it shapes the work even if it can't be discussed

### Information that Must Reach Jeremy

**Even from private conversations, the Not-Me surfaces:**

| What | How | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| Risk patterns | Generic alert | "Client may have concerns about [area] based on private context" |
| Opportunity patterns | General signal | "There may be additional scope based on undisclosed needs" |
| Relationship dynamics | Abstract pattern | "Political dynamics are more complex than presented" |
| Timeline pressures | General flag | "There may be urgency not formally communicated" |
| Trust concerns | Careful note | "Client needs extra care around [area]" |

**What Never Gets Shared Without Permission:**
- Specific statements or quotes
- Names of people mentioned
- Specific incidents described
- Any identifying details
- Anything that could be traced back
