## Phase 1: Formation (DONE or IN PROGRESS)

- [ ] **File Articles of Organization** with Colorado Secretary of State
  - Online: [Colorado Secretary of State](https://www.sos.state.co.us/pubs/business/businessHome.html)
  - Fee: ~$50
  - Processing: Same day if online

- [ ] **Get FEIN (Federal Employer Identification Number)** from IRS
  - Online: [IRS EIN Application](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online)
  - Free
  - Instant if done online (M-F, 7am-10pm ET)
  - You need this even as a single-member LLC

---

## Phase 2: State & Local Registration

### Colorado State

- [ ] **Register with Colorado Department of Revenue**
  - For state income tax withholding (if you hire)
  - For sales tax (if selling taxable goods/services)
  - Online: [Colorado Revenue Online](https://www.colorado.gov/revenueonline)

- [ ] **Periodic Report** (Annual Report)
  - Due every year, anniversary of formation
  - Fee: ~$10
  - File at Colorado Secretary of State

### Denver (if operating from Denver)

- [ ] **Denver Business License**
  - Required if conducting business in Denver
  - Online: [Denver Business Licensing](https://www.denvergov.org/Government/Agencies-Departments-Offices/Agencies-Departments-Offices-Directory/Department-of-Excise-and-Licenses/Business-Licensing)
  - Fee varies by business type

- [ ] **Denver Occupational Privilege Tax (OPT)**
  - $4/month per employee + $5.75/month employer
  - Register with Denver Treasury Division

---

## Phase 3: Banking & Financial

- [ ] **Open Business Bank Account**
  - Bring: Articles of Organization, EIN letter, ID
  - Keep business finances SEPARATE from personal
  - Options: Chase, Wells Fargo, Novo (online), Mercury (tech-focused)

- [ ] **Get Business Credit Card** (optional but recommended)
  - Builds business credit
  - Keeps expenses separate
  - Options: Chase Ink, Amex Business, Capital One Spark

- [ ] **Set Up Accounting System**
  - QuickBooks, Wave (free), FreshBooks
  - Track income, expenses, invoices
  - Makes taxes easier

---

## Phase 4: If Hiring Employees

- [ ] **Register for Colorado Unemployment Insurance**
  - Colorado Department of Labor and Employment
  - Required before first employee
  - [CDLE Employer Services](https://cdle.colorado.gov/employers)

- [ ] **Workers' Compensation Insurance**
  - Required in Colorado if you have employees
  - Get quotes from: Pinnacol Assurance, Hartford, State Farm

- [ ] **I-9 Verification**
  - Verify employee eligibility to work
  - E-Verify (optional but recommended)

---

## Phase 5: If Operating as Consultant/Contractor

- [ ] **W-9 Form**
  - Have ready to provide to clients
  - They need your EIN for 1099 reporting

- [ ] **Standard Contract Template**
  - Scope of work, payment terms, IP ownership
  - Consider having attorney review once

- [ ] **Professional Liability Insurance** (E&O)
  - Recommended for consultants
  - Protects against claims of negligence
  - ~$500-1500/year for consultants

- [ ] **General Liability Insurance**
  - Protects against property damage, injury claims
  - Often required by clients
  - ~$400-800/year

---

## Phase 6: Tax Setup

- [ ] **Decide Tax Election**
  - Default: Single-member LLC taxed as sole proprietor (Schedule C)
  - Option: Elect S-Corp taxation (Form 2553) - beneficial if profit > $40K
  - Consult accountant before electing S-Corp

- [ ] **Quarterly Estimated Taxes**
  - Due: April 15, June 15, Sept 15, Jan 15
  - Federal: [IRS Direct Pay](https://www.irs.gov/payments/direct-pay)
  - Colorado: [Colorado Revenue Online](https://www.colorado.gov/revenueonline)
  - Set aside ~30% of profit for taxes

- [ ] **Get Accountant/CPA**
  - Worth it for business taxes
  - Can advise on S-Corp election, deductions
  - Expect $500-1500/year for simple LLC

---

## Phase 7: Optional But Recommended

- [ ] **Operating Agreement**
  - Even single-member LLCs should have one
  - Documents how the LLC operates
  - Helps maintain liability protection
  - Templates available online or have attorney draft

- [ ] **Registered Agent**
  - Colorado requires one
  - Can be yourself (home address becomes public)
  - Or use service (~$50-150/year) for privacy

- [ ] **Business Address**
  - Can use home address
  - Or get virtual office/mailbox for professionalism
  - Options: UPS Store, Regus, virtual office services

- [ ] **Domain & Professional Email**
  - yourname.com or businessname.com
  - Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 for professional email
  - ~$6-12/month
